The University of Michigan College of Pharmacy

Consulting Project

Project Overview

This project focused on the use of contextual inquiry and human-centered design methodologies as applied to a consulting model. Our team held client meetings, conducted research and semi-structured interviews, brainstormed solutions, and ultimately provided recommendations designed to target their identified problem.

Deliverables

Background Research Report | Competitive Analysis | Affinity Wall | Final Consulting Report | Presentation

Problem

The Building Incident Response Team (BIRT) within the College of Pharmacy was seeking a more efficient and reliable communication method in emergencies, particularly in a fire emergency. Their current communication method relied heavily on the safety manager as an information hub to collect and process all the information from BIRT members through text messages, which was both inefficient and invulnerable, especially when the safety manager was absent from the building.

Research

We conducted background research to understand the clientâ€™s problem in four different areas, including physical building environment, emergency communication, emergency psychology, and best practices. The team created three sets of interview protocols for the stakeholders and conducted six interviews with two building managers, two BIRT members, and two fire safety specialists. This was all in an effort to better understand the current process and its pain points. After each interview, our team came together and conducted interpretation sessions, which generated 300+ affinity notes. Finally, we constructed an affinity wall that identified themes from our interviews to aggregate all the information and help us draw conclusions and make recommendations.
